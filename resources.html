Resources and Documents

Certainly, here are some resources and documents related to juvenile delinquency:

Books:

"Juvenile Delinquency: Causes and Control" by Robert Agnew

"Understanding Juvenile Justice and Delinquency" by Marilyn D. McShane and Frank P. Williams III

Reports and Publication:

Office of Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention (OJJDP): The OJJDP provides various reports, publications, and resources on juvenile justice issues.

National Institute of Justice (NIJ) - Juvenile Justice: NIJ offers research and resources on juvenile justice topics.

Websites:

Juvenile Justice Information Exchange: JJIE provides news, research, and resources related to juvenile justice.

The Annie E. Casey Foundation - Juvenile Justice: This foundation focuses on improving outcomes for disadvantaged children, and their website has resources on juvenile justice issues.

Academic Journals:

"Journal of Juvenile Justice" - A peer-reviewed journal covering various aspects of juvenile justice.

"Youth Violence and Juvenile Justice" - A journal that explores the intersection of youth violence and the juvenile justice system.

Goverment Resources:

Bureau of Justice Statistics (BJS) - Juvenile Arrests: BJS provides statistical information on juvenile arrests and various other justice-related topics.

Online Courses:

Coursera - Juvenile Justice: Coursera offers various courses related to juvenile justice that you can enroll in for a more in-depth study.

Remember to check your local libraries, universities, and governmental websites for additional resources specific to your region or country. These resources cover a range of perspectives on juvenile delinquency, from research and statistics to practical interventions and policy discussions.
